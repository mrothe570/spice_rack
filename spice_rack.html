Spice Rack

A digital guide for common herbs and spices

Basil comes from tropical regions like central Africa and southeast Asia. It's used in dishes worldwide. It's a complex, aromatic herb that starts savory and ends sweet. You can often find basil used as one of the main ingredients in:

  • tomato sauce
  • pesto
  • ice cream

Dill comes from Eurasia and provides a grassy, licorice-like flavor. Commonly used in eastern European cooking, you can find dill in:

  • aloo suva bhaji
  • gravlax
  • Dillkott

Oregano is native to western and southwestern Eurasia. Commnly used in Mediterranean dishes, oregano has a peppery taste with some apparent bitterness. You can find oregano in:

  • pizza and pasta sauce
  • olive oil-based dishes
  • part of marinades for lamb, chicken, and beef

Parsley is another native Mediterranean herb that has been naturalized throughout Europe. Clean and peppery like oregano, it's commonly used as a garnish but you can also find it in:

  • stuffed shrimp
  • Chimchurri sauce
  • tabbouleh

Rosemary has been used since ancient Greek and Roman times. It has a minty, balsamic taste with a woody aftertaste. It stands up strong in recipes and retains this profile to imbue all your favorite dishes with its unique flavor like:

  • roasted potatoes
  • pasta dishes
  • roasted chickena

Sage has an earth and pronounced herbal flavor. Its complexity can reveal hints of mint and lemeon. Considered a fall herb, it's commonly used with:

  • pumpkin
  • squash
  • turkey

Thyme is sharp and minty. It's a little sweet and a little spicy. It's typically found in savory dishes to season:

  • roasted meat
  • vegetables
  • fish

Cloves have a rich history in both culinary and medicinal contexts. They were even the subject of some minor mythology as a plant with paranormal powers! Find them in:

  • rubs or marinades for meat
  • Vietnamese pho
  • German red cabbage

Cumin has a warm and earthy bittersweetness. It comes from ancient Egypt and Syria originally and is used extensively in Indian cuisine. Use it with in recipes with foods like:

  • lentils and chickpeas
  • rubs and marinades
  • curry powder

Ginger comes from southeast Asia and is commonly found in Chinese, Filipino, and Japanese cuisines. It's peppery and sweet and has even found a place in holiday dishes in America. Find it in:

  • pumpkin spice anything
  • orange chicken
  • ginger root tea

Mace is the skin or protective coating that wraps around its more known sister spice Nutmeg. If it's orange and red it's from Indonesia and if it's orange and yellow it's from Grenada. Mace has a sweet and warm flavor and is found in:

  • porridges
  • prepared drinks
  • Indian cuisine

Mustard comes from Egypt and features in Greek mythology just like cloves. It has a sharp tang and its yellow hue comes from turmeric added to give it spice and brightness. Find it in:

  • condiments like barbecue sauce
  • mustard greens
  • pickles and brines

Paprika, or ground chili peppers, features prominently in Mexican cuisine but gets its name from Hungarian origins. It was once used as decorative houseplants throughout Europe, but its sweet heat quicklly found its way into recipes like:

  • North Carolina pulled pork
  • Cajun-style seafood and gumbo
  • paella

Saffron is a sweet and floral herb that comes from Iran, Greece, Morocco, and India. Saffron is made from the stigmata of flowers and must be harvested by hand. Find this precious spice in:

  • saffron rice
  • bouillabaisse
  • moussaka
